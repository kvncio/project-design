Tabuk

The city of Tabuk, the headquarters of the Emirate of the Tabuk region 
and the largest city in northwestern 
Saudi Arabia and around it are some of the most important monuments in the Arabian Peninsula.
The Tabuk region is the northern gateway to the Arabian Peninsula , and a vital route for trade 
pilgrims and Umrah performers from outside the Arabian Peninsula. It is one of the important agricultural 
areas in the Kingdom, and the city of Tabuk is specifically located 700 kilometers north of Medina , 
and 1,350 kilometers from the Riyadh region. 
It is also located on a low plain and its height above sea level is between 600-800 metres
